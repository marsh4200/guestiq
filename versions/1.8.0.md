## v1.8.0 — Backup & restore

New **Backup & restore** section at the bottom of Settings (administrators
only — a backup contains guest details and password hashes).

### What's in a backup
Everything: rooms, guest records, every stay and its history, hotel settings,
the uploaded logo, staff accounts and their permissions. It comes out as a
single `.zip` holding a readable JSON export plus the logo file, so you can
open it and see exactly what's in there.

### Backing up
- **Download backup** — one file, keep it off-site.
- **Save a copy on the server** — kept in `data/backups/`, which is
  bind-mounted and survives rebuilds.
- **Daily automatic backup** (on by default) with a configurable number to
  keep — the oldest automatic copies are pruned, manual and pre-restore copies
  never are.
- The table lists every copy on the server with when it was taken, what's in
  it and its size, with Download / Restore / Delete per row.

### Restoring
Pick a `.zip` and GuestIQ reads it *before* touching anything, then shows what
it contains — rooms, guests, stays, accounts — and asks you to confirm.

- **A safety copy of the current data is written first**, so restoring the
  wrong file can be undone.
- Tick box to include or skip staff accounts and passwords — leave it off to
  restore a property's data without overwriting who can sign in.
- Everyone is signed out afterwards, since accounts may have changed.
- Version-tolerant: a backup from an older GuestIQ restores onto a newer one.
  Unknown columns are ignored, new ones take their defaults.
- If anything goes wrong mid-restore the whole thing rolls back and the
  database is left exactly as it was.

### API (administrators only)
- `GET /api/backup/download` · `GET /api/backup/list` · `POST /api/backup/create`
- `GET|DELETE /api/backup/file/{name}` · `POST /api/backup/restore/{name}`
- `POST /api/backup/inspect` · `POST /api/backup/restore` (multipart, with
  `?include_users=`)
