## v1.5.2 — Occupancy drives every device in the room, not just the geyser

### Wording
- Automation tab now says what actually happens: checking a guest in tells the
  hub the room is occupied, so **geyser, lights, TV, air-con, underfloor
  heating and anything else smart in the room** wakes up. Check-out switches it
  all back.
- Checkout dialog reworded to match.
- New explainer panel on the Automation tab describing what the hub receives.

### Richer occupancy payload
Occupancy events used to carry only `room` and `occupied`, which is enough for
a geyser and not much else. They now also carry:

| field | meaning |
|---|---|
| `event` | `occupied` / `vacated` |
| `num_guests` | how many people are in the room |
| `due_out` | the booked check-out date/time |

So hub-side automations can dim lights to the guest count, pre-heat before
arrival, kill the TV and air-con at check-out, or run a housekeeping scene when
the room empties — without GuestIQ needing to know about individual devices.

Additive only: `room` and `occupied` are unchanged and in the same place, so
existing load-manager automations keep working untouched.
